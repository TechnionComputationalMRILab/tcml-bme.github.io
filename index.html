Welcome to the
Technion Computational MRI Lab

Dec. 20, 2020: Exciting News! The TCML was awarded a grant from the Israel Innovation Authority to continue our research developing a 3D processing technique for automatic virtual unfolding and assessing disease extent in Magnetic Resonance Enterography data of Crohn’s disease patients in collaboration with Dr. Anat Ilivitzka at Rambam.
Dec 20, 2020: Exciting News! As part of a multinational project and in partnership with the Technion AIMLab, we were awarded an ERA-CVD grant on the topic of ENRICH: ENdothelial Retinal function as Indicator for vascular Cognitive Health.
December 15, 2020: Congratulations! Samah Khawaled was awarded the Ariane de Rothschild Women’s Doctoral Program scholarship granted to talented and outstanding female PhD students. Samah was one of 4 awardees from the Technion.
December 2020: Congratulations! Samah Khawaled had her abstract on Deformable Image Registration: A Bayesian Framework accepted to the Medical Imaging meets NeurIPS workshop. See here.
August 20, 2020: Congratulations! Yael Zaffrani was awarded 2nd place in the Technion's Undergrad Projects Day Competition for the work she did on building a MRI Visualization Tool for Crohn’s Disease Assessment. Check out the announcement (in Hebrew) on the Technion's webpage here.
June 15, 2020: Exciting News! We were awarded a BSF Grant enabling a collaboration between our lab and the CRL lab at Harvard Medical School.

Improving Patient Care

Developing Novel Deep-Learning-Based Methods for MRI Acquisition and Analysis

We are improving patient care through better characterization of the underlying physiological and structural factors in human diseases by developing novel deep-learning-based methods for MRI acquisition and analysis.

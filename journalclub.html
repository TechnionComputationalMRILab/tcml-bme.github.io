Journal Club Participation Guidelines:

The Journal Club is a weekly meeting where participants come together to discuss key concepts and new methods shared in relevant journal articles. Participants are expected to take turns selecting a journal article for an upcoming meeting. All participants are to carefully read the selected article prior to the meeting and be prepared to disuss the research in depth.

Below you will find suggested methods and questions to help you prepare for the meetings.

Guidelines for annotating within the text:

- Don't underline everything! Be thoughtful as to what information is most important.
- See if you can answer the question you wrote by the heading when you are done reading.
- Try and summarize the article succinctly (in a breif clear manner). No more then a few sentences.


Some questions to think about and answer:

  • What is the research/method accomplishing? (What is the point?)
  • How can the research/method be improved?
  • What are next steps the researchers can take to expand the concept? How might the research/method be applicable?
  • What was innovative about the research/method?
  • Which databases were used for the experiment (are the public or private)? Which algorithms/networks were used?
